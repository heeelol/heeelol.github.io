🇨🇳 ZHEJIANG UNIVERSITY · WINTER EXCHANGE

WINTER IN HANGZHOU

DEC 2025 2½ WEEKS DIGITAL DESIGN & LOGIC +5 KG, NO REGRETS

For two and a half weeks in December 2025, I traded Singapore's dependable 31°C for a winter exchange at Zhejiang University. The deal: a digital design course by day, a borrowed bicycle for everything else, and a personal mission to eat my way across Hangzhou. I came home with a head full of Verilog and roughly five extra kilograms. Absolutely worth it.

It Was Cold. Genuinely Cold. 寒い

Let me set the scene for my fellow equator-dwellers. Some days never climbed above 10°C, and a few flirted dangerously close to zero. As a Singaporean whose body considers air-conditioning "a bit much," I took this as a personal attack.

I learned what "layering" actually means. I formed a deep, almost spiritual bond with hot soup. But there's something magic about a cold, clear winter evening — the light turns gold, the streets glow, and suddenly the shivering feels like a fair trade.

8AM: Logic Gates & a Sleeping Professor 論理

Most mornings started early — dragging myself out of a very warm bed for Digital Design & Logic. We built up from humble AND, OR and NOT gates all the way to writing real working hardware in Verilog. Watching abstract truth tables turn into something that actually does things never stopped being satisfying.

The undisputed highlight, though, had nothing to do with the syllabus. During one lesson, our professor quietly dozed off mid-class — and then began to sleep-talk. Still lecturing, somewhere in his dreams. We didn't have the heart to wake him. Honestly? The most relatable thing a teacher has ever done.

“The professor fell asleep and kept teaching. Dedication, technically.”

A Campus the Size of a Small Country 大学

ZJU's campus is enormous — so sprawling that there's genuinely no internal bus service. The local solution is wonderfully simple: rent a bike and pedal everywhere. Some of my favourite moments of the whole trip were exactly this — cruising across campus, cold breeze biting at my face, gliding past a gorgeous lake sitting right in the middle of it all.

Trivia for the collectors: of Zhejiang University's four campuses, this was the oldest one — decades of history under every wheel.

Worth Every One of Those Five Kilos 美食

Okay. The food. The food. Everything was bursting with flavour in a way that made me quietly re-evaluate my entire palate. Beef noodles that ruined other beef noodles for me forever. Fried chicken worthy of its own fan club. Desserts I would happily cross a province for. I even tried pork organs for the first time — verdict: braver than expected, tastier than feared.

My single fondest food memory was a little green tea restaurant, where the fried chicken and dessert were so good I still think about them on a roughly weekly basis. I boarded the flight home five kilograms heavier and not even slightly sorry.

“Came for the exchange. Stayed for the fried chicken.”

Lakes That Made Me Stop Walking 風景

The scenery did most of the heavy lifting for my camera roll. West Lake at the right hour is the kind of view that makes you go quiet for a moment and just look. The matcha lake — ringed by tea-green forest — was every bit as breathtaking. I'll spare you the full, embarrassingly large collection of sunset photos and show only a tasteful few.

A Detour to the Zoo 動物園

With the course winding down, the last few days in Hangzhou freed up for something gloriously unacademic: the zoo. I made the rounds and said hello to the locals — fish gliding around in slow motion, a magnificently unbothered panda, a snake that held eye contact a beat too long, and a seal that was clearly the showman of the bunch. Ten out of ten. Would gawk again.

How a Car Gets Made 工場

We also went on a field trip to a car factory — a front-row seat to how a pile of parts becomes an actual vehicle. Watching the assembly line move in that synchronised, almost choreographed way is weirdly mesmerising: robotic arms swinging in unison, millimetre precision, not a wasted motion in sight. The engineer in me was very, very happy. I grabbed a short clip — watch the arms do their thing.

Weekends Were for Wandering 上海

Classes wrapped up around 4pm most days, and weekends were entirely ours — which, naturally, meant travel and sightseeing. The standout: a day trip to Shanghai on a bullet train. I knew, intellectually, that it would be fast. I was not emotionally prepared for how fast. Blink and a city's gone by.

Shanghai delivered on every front — the skyline, the Shanghai Tower punching through the clouds, jaw-dropping architecture and golden statues around every corner, and a coffee with a frankly unfair view. A very good way to spend a day off.

Best plot twist of the trip: Shanghai has a life-sized Freedom Gundam replica. I'd photographed one before at DiverCity in Tokyo, so lining up the matching shot here felt like bumping into an old friend in a new city — one that happens to be a towering mobile suit. Two countries, same nerd, same pose. The collection continues.

Two and a half weeks. One digital design course, several unforgettable lakes, far too much excellent food, and one professor's dream-lecture I'll be quoting for years. Hangzhou, you were unexpectedly, thoroughly wonderful. 再见 — until next time. ✌️

THE WHOLE CREW. 谢谢 FOR THE MEMORIES.

FIELD NOTES BY YAP JIA WEI · HANGZHOU · DEC 2025
